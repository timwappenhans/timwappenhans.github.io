Two correct winners from three. England needed rescuing: Brian Cipenga put DR Congo ahead inside seven minutes and the outsiders led until Harry Kane struck twice in the last fifteen (a 75th-minute header, then an 86th-minute finish) to spare the Three Lions a shock. My miss came in Seattle, where I had backed Senegal: they led Belgium 2-0 into the 86th minute through Habib Diarra and Ismaïla Sarr before Romelu Lukaku and Youri Tielemans levelled in three minutes to force extra time, and Tielemans converted a hotly disputed VAR penalty deep in stoppage time to win it 3-2. The USA came through despite Folarin Balogun's controversial 64th-minute red card, Balogun having opened the scoring on halftime and Malik Tillman curling in a superb free kick to seal a 2-0 win with ten men. Across the tournament my record stands at 47 correct winners from 74, revised down from 48 in today's audit (see the correction below).

A correction before the new slate: this morning's edition paired the wrong teams in two of today's cards. The same bad data had crept into earlier results: the verified June 27-30 scores are now on the results page, including Paraguay and Morocco eliminating Germany and the Netherlands on penalties, and Mexico, not Ecuador, winning at the Azteca. Re-scored against real results, my tally drops one winner to 47 of 74; the consolation is two exact scores I was never credited for (France 3-0 Sweden, Ivory Coast 1-2 Norway).
