How teams get here: 48 teams, 12 groups of four. The top two of every group qualify directly (24 teams), and the eight best of the twelve third-placed teams fill the rest, for a 32-team Round of 32, the first in World Cup history. Four third-placed teams go home. The group stage is complete and all 32 places are filled. The three final groups settled on June 27: England and Croatia advanced from Group L, Colombia and Portugal from Group K, and Argentina and Austria from Group J. The eight best third-placed teams are confirmed - DR Congo (K), Ecuador (E), Sweden (F), Ghana (L), Bosnia & Herzegovina (B), Algeria (J) and Paraguay (D), the seven thirds on four points, plus Senegal (I), the best of the three-point thirds. Iran, South Korea, Scotland and Uruguay are the four thirds that missed out. Each third has now been slotted to its winner per FIFA's published allocation table, so every blue placeholder below resolves to a named team. The Round of 32 is under way: Canada beat South Africa 1-0 in the Los Angeles opener on June 28; on June 29 Paraguay stunned Germany 4-3 on penalties after a 1-1 draw (Germany's first-ever World Cup shootout defeat), Morocco beat the Netherlands 3-2 on penalties after 1-1, and Brazil edged Japan 2-1; on June 30 France beat Sweden 3-0, Norway won 2-1 against Ivory Coast, and co-hosts Mexico beat Ecuador 2-0 at the Azteca; and on July 1 England (2-1 vs DR Congo, on a late Kane brace), the United States (2-0 vs Bosnia, playing over half an hour a man down) and Belgium (2-2, 3-2 after extra time vs Senegal, a stoppage-time VAR penalty settling a stunning comeback) came through. On July 2 Portugal came from behind to beat Croatia 2-1 (a Goncalo Ramos header in the 94th minute, ending Modric's career), Spain eased past Austria 3-0, and Switzerland beat Algeria 2-0 for their first World Cup knockout win in 88 years. On July 3 the Round of 32 closed: Egypt beat Australia 4-2 on penalties after a 1-1 draw for their first-ever World Cup knockout win, Argentina survived a 3-2 extra-time scare against debutants Cape Verde, and Colombia beat Ghana 1-0. All eight Round-of-16 ties are now set: Paraguay vs France in Philadelphia (M89), Canada vs Morocco in Houston (M90), Brazil vs Norway in New Jersey (M91), Mexico vs England in Mexico City (M92), Portugal vs Spain in Dallas (M93), the United States vs Belgium in Seattle (M94), Argentina vs Egypt in Atlanta (M95), and Switzerland vs Colombia in Vancouver (M96). The Round of 16 is under way: on July 4 France beat Paraguay 1-0 on a Kylian Mbappe penalty and Morocco beat co-hosts Canada 3-0 on an Azzedine Ounahi double and a late Rahimi third, setting the first quarterfinal, France vs Morocco in Boston (M97) on July 9. On July 5 Norway stunned Brazil 2-1 on an Erling Haaland second-half brace (Neymar replying from the spot in stoppage time) to reach their first-ever quarterfinal, and ten-man England beat co-hosts Mexico 3-2 at the Azteca on a Jude Bellingham brace and a Harry Kane penalty, despite Jarell Quansah's red card, handing Mexico their first home World Cup defeat; that resolves the third quarterfinal, Norway vs England in Miami (M99) on July 11. On July 6 the top half closed: Spain beat Portugal 1-0 on Mikel Merino's stoppage-time winner (90+1'), likely the last World Cup night of the 41-year-old Cristiano Ronaldo, and Belgium overran co-hosts United States 4-1 (Charles De Ketelaere twice), resolving the second quarterfinal, Spain vs Belgium in Los Angeles (M98) on July 10. On July 7 the last 16 was completed: Argentina came from 2-0 down to beat Egypt 3-2 on an Enzo Fernandez header in stoppage time, and Switzerland beat Colombia 4-3 on penalties after a goalless 120 minutes, filling the last quarterfinal, Argentina vs Switzerland in Kansas City (M100) on July 11. All four quarterfinals are now set: France vs Morocco in Boston (M97, July 9), Spain vs Belgium in Los Angeles (M98, July 10), Norway vs England in Miami (M99, July 11), and Argentina vs Switzerland in Kansas City (M100, July 11). The quarterfinals are under way: France beat Morocco 2-0 in Boston on July 9 (Mbappe 60', Dembele 66', with Mbappe's first-half penalty saved by Bounou), a rematch of the 2022 semifinal, to become the first side into the last four, and Spain beat Belgium 2-1 in Los Angeles on July 10 (Fabian Ruiz 30', Merino 88'; De Ketelaere 41', the first goal Spain conceded all tournament) to join them, setting the first semifinal, France vs Spain in Dallas (M101) on July 14. The quarterfinals are now complete: on July 11 England beat Norway 2-1 after extra time in Miami (1-1 at 90; Schjelderup 36' for Norway, Bellingham 45+' and 93') and Argentina beat Switzerland 3-1 after extra time in Kansas City (1-1 at 90; Mac Allister 10', Ndoye 67' for Switzerland, Alvarez 112', Lautaro Martinez 120+1', with Switzerland's Breel Embolo sent off). Both semifinals are now set: France vs Spain in Dallas (M101) on July 14, and England vs Argentina in Atlanta (M102) on July 15. The first semifinal is decided: Spain beat France 2-0 in Dallas on July 14 (Oyarzabal penalty around 21', Porro 58', with a Lamine Yamal goal ruled out for offside just after the hour and no extra time), reaching a second World Cup final; France, beaten for the first time all tournament, drop into the third-place game in Miami on July 18. The final is set: Argentina came from behind to beat England 2-1 in Atlanta on July 15 (Gordon 55' for England; Enzo Fernandez 85', Lautaro Martinez 90+2', both from Messi passes, and no extra time), so the holders meet Spain in the final at MetLife Stadium on July 19 (M104), while England join France in the third-place game in Miami on July 18 (M103).
