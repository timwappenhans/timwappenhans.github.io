How teams get here: 48 teams, 12 groups of four. The top two of every group qualify directly (24 teams), and the eight best of the twelve third-placed teams fill the rest, for a 32-team Round of 32, the first in World Cup history. Four third-placed teams go home. The group stage is complete and all 32 places are filled. The three final groups settled on June 27: England and Croatia advanced from Group L, Portugal and Colombia from Group K, and Argentina and Austria from Group J. The eight best third-placed teams are confirmed - DR Congo (K), Ecuador (E), Sweden (F), Ghana (L), Bosnia & Herzegovina (B), Algeria (J) and Paraguay (D), the seven thirds on four points, plus Senegal (I), the best of the three-point thirds. Iran, South Korea, Scotland and Uruguay are the four thirds that missed out. Each third has now been slotted to its winner per FIFA's published allocation table, so every blue placeholder below resolves to a named team. The Round of 32 is under way: Canada beat South Africa 0-1 in the Los Angeles opener on June 28; on June 29 Germany (3-1 vs Paraguay), the Netherlands (2-1 vs Morocco) and Brazil (2-1 vs Japan) advanced; and on June 30 France (2-0 vs Sweden), Norway (3-1 vs Ivory Coast) and Ecuador (1-1, 4-2 on penalties vs co-hosts Mexico) went through. Three Round-of-16 ties are now set: Germany vs France in Philadelphia (M89), Canada vs Netherlands in Houston (M90), and Brazil vs Norway in New Jersey (M91), with Ecuador (M92) awaiting the winner of England vs DR Congo. Six Round-of-32 ties remain, July 1 to 3.
