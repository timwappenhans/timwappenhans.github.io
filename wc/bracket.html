How teams get here: 48 teams, 12 groups of four. The top two of every group qualify directly (24 teams), and the eight best of the twelve third-placed teams fill the rest, for a 32-team Round of 32, the first in World Cup history. Four third-placed teams go home. The group stage is complete and all 32 places are filled. The three final groups settled on June 27: England and Croatia advanced from Group L, Colombia and Portugal from Group K, and Argentina and Austria from Group J. The eight best third-placed teams are confirmed - DR Congo (K), Ecuador (E), Sweden (F), Ghana (L), Bosnia & Herzegovina (B), Algeria (J) and Paraguay (D), the seven thirds on four points, plus Senegal (I), the best of the three-point thirds. Iran, South Korea, Scotland and Uruguay are the four thirds that missed out. Each third has now been slotted to its winner per FIFA's published allocation table, so every blue placeholder below resolves to a named team. The Round of 32 is under way: Canada beat South Africa 1-0 in the Los Angeles opener on June 28; on June 29 Paraguay stunned Germany 4-3 on penalties after a 1-1 draw (Germany's first-ever World Cup shootout defeat), Morocco beat the Netherlands 3-2 on penalties after 1-1, and Brazil edged Japan 2-1; on June 30 France beat Sweden 3-0, Norway won 2-1 against Ivory Coast, and co-hosts Mexico beat Ecuador 2-0 at the Azteca; and on July 1 England (2-1 vs DR Congo, on a late Kane brace), the United States (2-0 vs Bosnia, playing over half an hour a man down) and Belgium (2-2, 3-2 after extra time vs Senegal, a stoppage-time VAR penalty settling a stunning comeback) came through. On July 2 Portugal came from behind to beat Croatia 2-1 (a Goncalo Ramos header in the 94th minute, ending Modric's career), Spain eased past Austria 3-0, and Switzerland beat Algeria 2-0 for their first World Cup knockout win in 88 years. On July 3 the Round of 32 closed: Egypt beat Australia 4-2 on penalties after a 1-1 draw for their first-ever World Cup knockout win, Argentina survived a 3-2 extra-time scare against debutants Cape Verde, and Colombia beat Ghana 1-0. All eight Round-of-16 ties are now set: Paraguay vs France in Philadelphia (M89), Canada vs Morocco in Houston (M90), Brazil vs Norway in New Jersey (M91), Mexico vs England in Mexico City (M92), Portugal vs Spain in Dallas (M93), the United States vs Belgium in Seattle (M94), Argentina vs Egypt in Atlanta (M95), and Switzerland vs Colombia in Vancouver (M96). The Round of 16 is under way: on July 4 France beat Paraguay 1-0 on a Kylian Mbappe penalty and Morocco beat co-hosts Canada 3-0 on an Azzedine Ounahi double and a late Rahimi third, setting the first quarterfinal, France vs Morocco in Boston (M97) on July 9. The last 16 continues July 5 with Brazil vs Norway (New Jersey) and Mexico vs England (Mexico City).
