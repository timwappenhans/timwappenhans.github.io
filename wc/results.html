Colombia won Group K after a breathless 0-0 with Portugal in Miami Gardens, Davinson Sánchez's stoppage-time header disallowed by VAR for offside; Portugal took second on five points, and DR Congo beat Uzbekistan 3-1 (Wissa 68' pen, Mayele 78', Wissa 90+1'; Shomurodov 10') to finish third on four points and reach the knockouts for the first time. Argentina completed a perfect group with a 3-1 win over Jordan (Lo Celso 19', Lautaro Martínez 31' pen, Messi 80' free kick off the bench, a record seventh consecutive World Cup match with a goal); Algeria and Austria played out a wild 3-3 (Belghali 45', Mahrez 60', 90+3'; Arnautović 28', Sabitzer 55', Kalajdžić 90+6'), the 96th-minute equalizer sending Austria through second on goal difference with Algeria third. The group stage is complete: the eight best third-placed teams are Bosnia & Herzegovina, Paraguay, Ecuador, Sweden, Algeria, DR Congo, Ghana and Senegal; Iran, South Korea, Scotland and Uruguay miss out. Knockout stage · Round of 32

Single elimination from here. A draw after 90 minutes goes to extra time and, if still level, penalties. Winners advance to the Round of 16 (July 4 to 7).

Sunday, June 28 · Round of 32

R32South Africa0 : 1Canada

Canada are the first team into the Round of 16. Stephen Eustáquio's stoppage-time half-volley (90+2') settled a tense, low-scoring runners-up derby at SoFi Stadium that had looked bound for extra time. It was Canada's first-ever World Cup knockout win; South Africa, in their first knockout tie, go out at the first hurdle. Alphonso Davies returned from his hamstring layoff off the bench. Canada next meet the winner of Netherlands vs Morocco in Houston on July 4. Three more round-of-32 ties follow on June 29: Netherlands vs Morocco, Brazil vs Japan, and Germany vs Paraguay.

Monday, June 29 · Round of 32

R32Netherlands1 : 1Morocco
R32Brazil2 : 1Japan
R32Germany1 : 1Paraguay

Two penalty-shootout shocks. Morocco knocked out the Netherlands 3-2 on penalties after a 1-1 draw in Monterrey, Cody Gakpo's 72nd-minute opener canceled out by Sofyan Diop in stoppage time (90+1'); Morocco meet Canada in Houston on July 4. Brazil edged a dangerous Japan 2-1 in Houston, Casemiro (56') and a stoppage-time Martinelli winner (90+5') overturning Keito Sano's early opener (29'); Brazil next face the winner of Ivory Coast vs Norway. And the night's earthquake came at Gillette Stadium: Paraguay eliminated Germany 4-3 on penalties after 1-1 (Enciso 42'; Havertz 54'), Orlando Gill saving from Havertz and Woltemade and Jonathan Tah firing over before José Canale converted the decisive kick. It was the first penalty-shootout defeat in Germany's World Cup history; Paraguay go on to meet the winner of France vs Sweden in Philadelphia on July 4. The Round of 32 continues June 30 with France vs Sweden, Ivory Coast vs Norway, and Mexico vs Ecuador.

Tuesday, June 30 · Round of 32

R32Ivory Coast1 : 2Norway
R32France3 : 0Sweden
R32Mexico2 : 0Ecuador

Three more places in the Round of 16 were settled. Norway beat the Ivory Coast 2-1 in Dallas, Antonio Nusa (39') and Erling Haaland (86') either side of Amad Diallo's equalizer (74'); Norway go on to meet Brazil in New Jersey on July 5. France were emphatic 3-0 winners over Sweden at MetLife Stadium, Kylian Mbappé striking twice (45', 74') around a Bradley Barcola goal (53') to set up a Round-of-16 tie with Paraguay in Philadelphia on July 4. And the co-hosts marched on at the Estadio Azteca: Mexico beat Ecuador 2-0 to reach the last 16 on home soil, where England await on July 5. The Round of 32 continues July 1 with England vs DR Congo, Belgium vs Senegal, and USA vs Bosnia & Herzegovina.

Wednesday, July 1 · Round of 32

R32England2 : 1DR Congo
R32Belgium2 : 2Senegal
R32United States2 : 0Bosnia & Herzegovina

Three more sides reached the Round of 16, two of them the hard way. England came from behind to beat DR Congo 2-1 in Atlanta, Brian Cipenga's seventh-minute strike putting the outsiders ahead before Harry Kane rescued the Three Lions with two goals in the last fifteen minutes (75' header, 86'); England go on to face co-hosts Mexico at the Estadio Azteca on July 5. Belgium produced the comeback of the tournament in Seattle, trailing Senegal 2-0 (Habib Diarra 25', Ismaïla Sarr 51') into the 86th minute before Romelu Lukaku (86') and Youri Tielemans (89') levelled in three minutes to force extra time, where Tielemans converted a hotly disputed VAR penalty deep in stoppage time (120+5') to win it 3-2; Senegal go out having squandered a commanding lead. The United States beat Bosnia & Herzegovina 2-0 at Levi's Stadium despite playing over half an hour a man down: Folarin Balogun scored on the stroke of halftime (45') but was sent off after a VAR review on 64 minutes, and ten-man USA still added a second through Malik Tillman's superb free kick (82') to book a last-16 tie with Belgium in Seattle on July 6; Balogun is suspended for that game. The Round of 32 concludes July 2 (Portugal vs Croatia, Spain vs Austria, Switzerland vs Algeria) and July 3 (Argentina vs Cape Verde, Colombia vs Ghana, Australia vs Egypt).

Thursday, July 2 · Round of 32

R32Portugal2 : 1Croatia
R32Spain3 : 0Austria
R32Switzerland2 : 0Algeria

Three more sides reached the Round of 16 and the last-16 draw is nearly complete. Portugal came from behind to beat Croatia 2-1 in Toronto in a tie that ended Luka Modric's international career: Ivan Perisic put Croatia ahead early in the second half (53'), Cristiano Ronaldo levelled from the penalty spot for his first World Cup knockout goal (68'), and substitute Goncalo Ramos headed the winner deep in stoppage time (90+4') from a Rafael Leao cross; Croatia thought they had forced extra time in the dying moments, but Mario Pasalic was ruled offside by VAR. Spain were comfortable 3-0 winners over Austria at SoFi Stadium, Mikel Oyarzabal scoring twice (36', 89') around a Pedro Porro header (66') as the European champions advanced without conceding a goal all tournament. Switzerland beat Algeria 2-0 in Vancouver for their first World Cup knockout win in 88 years, Breel Embolo finishing a Johan Manzambi counter (10') and Dan Ndoye adding a second right after the break (46') past Luca Zidane. That sets the sixth Round-of-16 tie, Portugal vs Spain in Dallas on July 6; Switzerland face the Colombia vs Ghana winner in Vancouver on July 7. The Round of 32 concludes July 3 with Australia vs Egypt, Argentina vs Cape Verde, and Colombia vs Ghana.

Friday, July 3 · Round of 32

R32Australia1 : 1Egypt
R32Argentina1 : 1Cape Verde
R32Colombia1 : 0Ghana

The Round of 32 is complete. Egypt beat Australia on penalties in Dallas, a 1-1 draw (Emam Ashour 13'; Mohamed Hany own goal 55') going the distance before Egypt won the shootout 4-2, Mohamed Salah chipping in a Panenka and Hossam Abdelmaguid converting the decider after Harry Souttar and Lucas Herrington missed; it was Egypt's first-ever World Cup knockout win, and they meet Argentina in Atlanta on July 7. Argentina survived a huge scare at Hard Rock Stadium, beating debutants Cape Verde 3-2 after extra time (1-1 at 90): Lionel Messi opened on 29' for a record-extending 20th World Cup goal, Deroy Duarte levelled (59'), Lisandro Martínez restored the lead early in extra time (92'), Sidny Lopes Cabral made it 2-2 with a stunner (103'), and a Diney Borges own goal (111') off a Cristian Romero header settled it. Colombia beat Ghana 1-0 in Kansas City on Jhon Arias's 14th-minute finish from a Luis Suárez cross, Ghana failing to register a shot in the first 90 minutes and going out; Colombia face Switzerland in Vancouver on July 7. That completes the Round of 32 and sets the last two Round-of-16 ties: Argentina vs Egypt (Atlanta, July 7) and Switzerland vs Colombia (Vancouver, July 7). The Round of 16 opens July 4 with Canada vs Morocco (Houston) and Paraguay vs France (Philadelphia).
