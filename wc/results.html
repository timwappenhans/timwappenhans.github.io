Groups C and D have completed matchday 2. The United States beat Australia 2-0 (Burgess own goal 11', Freeman 43') to clinch a round-of-32 place and move top of Group D, becoming the second team into the knockouts after Mexico. Morocco edged Scotland 1-0 on Ismael Saibari's early goal to go top of Group C. Brazil claimed their first win, 3-0 over Haiti (Cunha 23', 36'; Vinícius Júnior 45+3'). Groups E and F have completed matchday 2. The Netherlands went top of Group F with a 5-1 rout of Sweden, Brian Brobbey (5', 17') and Cody Gakpo (47', 54') each scoring twice and Summerville adding a fifth, Elanga replying for Sweden. Germany came from behind to beat Ivory Coast 2-1 and clinch a round-of-32 place, substitute Deniz Undav scoring twice (68', 90+4') after Franck Kessié had put the Elephants ahead; Felix Nmecha went off injured in the first half. Ecuador and Curaçao drew 0-0, Curaçao keeper Eloy Room making around 15 saves for the smallest nation at the finals to earn their first-ever World Cup point against a wasteful Ecuador. Japan thrashed Tunisia 4-0 (Kamada 4', Ueda 31', 83', Ito 68') to move level with the Netherlands at the top, eliminating a winless Tunisia. Matchday 2 continues with Groups G and H on June 21.

HSpain4 : 0Saudi Arabia

Groups G and H have completed matchday 2. Spain ended their goal drought emphatically, beating Saudi Arabia 4-0 (Lamine Yamal 11' for his first World Cup goal, Mikel Oyarzabal 21' and 24', and a Hassan Al-Tambakti own goal 49') to go top of Group H. Belgium and Iran drew 0-0, Iran keeper Alireza Beiranvand starring and Belgium's Nathan Ngoy sent off on 66 minutes. Cape Verde struck twice through Kevin Pina's long-range free kick and a Hélio Varela lob to earn a 2-2 draw with Uruguay, Maxi Araújo and Agustín Canobbio replying for a wasteful Uruguay. Egypt came from behind to beat New Zealand 3-1, Mohamed Salah becoming Egypt's all-time World Cup top scorer after Finn Surman had headed the All Whites ahead. Matchday 2 concludes with Groups I and J on June 22, then Groups K and L on June 23.

JArgentina2 : 0Austria

Groups I and J have completed matchday 2. Argentina beat Austria 2-0 to clinch the knockouts and top spot, Lionel Messi scoring twice after missing an early penalty to overtake Miroslav Klose as the all-time men's World Cup top scorer on 18 goals. France beat Iraq 3-0 (Mbappé two, taking him level with Klose's old mark on 16, and Dembélé) through a two-hour halftime thunderstorm delay, qualifying alongside Norway, who edged Senegal 3-2 (Holmgren Pedersen, Haaland two; Ismaila Sarr two). Algeria came from behind to beat Jordan 2-1, both goals from Riyad Mahrez corners (Benbouali, Gouiri) after Nizar Al-Rashdan had put debutants Jordan ahead, eliminating Jordan. Matchday 2 concludes with Groups K and L on June 23.

Tuesday, June 23 · matchday 2 (Groups K, L)

KPortugal5 : 0Uzbekistan
LEngland0 : 0Ghana
LPanama0 : 1Croatia

Groups K and L have completed matchday 2, closing out the second round of group games. Portugal routed Uzbekistan 5-0 (Ronaldo two, Nuno Mendes, a Nematov own goal, Leão), Cristiano Ronaldo becoming the first man to score at six different World Cups and passing Eusébio as Portugal's all-time World Cup top scorer. England and Ghana drew 0-0 in a flat game, Harry Kane missing an open goal. Croatia revived their campaign with a 1-0 win over Panama on substitute Ante Budimir's goal, eliminating Panama, while Colombia sealed qualification with a 1-0 win over DR Congo on Daniel Muñoz's deflected 76th-minute strike. Matchday 3, the final group round, begins June 24 with Groups B, C and A.

Groups B, C and A have completed matchday 3. Switzerland won Group B with a 2-1 comeback over Canada (Vargas 46', Manzambi 57'; Promise David 76'), finishing unbeaten on seven points; Canada took second and Bosnia third after Bosnia beat Qatar 3-1 (18-year-old Kerim Alajbegović 29', a Sultan Al-Brake own goal 34', Mahmić 80'; Al-Haydos 42'), with both Canada and Bosnia on four points. Brazil topped Group C, beating Scotland 3-0 (Vinícius Júnior 7', 45+3', Cunha 60'), and Morocco took second with a 4-2 win over Haiti after twice trailing (own goal 10', Hakimi 39', Saibari 45+1', Rahimi 78', Yassine 89'; Joseph and Isidor 43' for Haiti). Mexico completed a perfect group with three clean sheets, beating Czechia 3-0 (Chávez 55', Quiñones 61', Fidalgo 90+4'), while South Africa shocked South Korea 1-0 (Maseko 63') to take second in Group A for their first-ever World Cup knockout place, dropping South Korea to third. Matchday 3 continues with Groups D, E and F on June 25.
