Groups I and J have completed matchday 2. Algeria came from behind to beat Jordan 2-1, both goals from Riyad Mahrez corners (Benbouali, Gouiri) after Nizar Al-Rashdan had put debutants Jordan ahead, eliminating Jordan. Groups G, H and I have completed matchday 3. France won Group I with a perfect nine points, thrashing Norway 4-1 on Ousmane Dembélé's first-half hat-trick (7', 20', 32'), Désiré Doué adding a late fourth; Thelo Aasgaard replied for Norway, who had a Jørgen Strand Larsen penalty saved by Mike Maignan but still went through second. Senegal hammered ten-man Iraq 5-0, the biggest World Cup win by an African nation (Habib Diarra 4', Ismaïla Sarr 56', Pape Gueye 60', 71', Iliman Ndiaye 82'), to take third and a best-third place; Iraq's Rebin Sulaka was sent off on 13 minutes. Spain won Group H by beating Uruguay 1-0 (Álex Baena 42'), eliminating the two-time champions, while Cape Verde drew 0-0 with Saudi Arabia to clinch a historic second place, becoming the smallest nation ever to reach the World Cup knockout stage. Belgium thrashed New Zealand 5-1 (Trossard 28', 50', De Bruyne 66', Lukaku 86', Saelemaekers 90+4'; Just for New Zealand) to leapfrog Egypt and top Group G; Egypt, held 1-1 by Iran (Saber 5'; Rezaeian 14'), took second after Iran had a stoppage-time winner ruled out for offside by VAR, leaving Iran third in the best-third race. Knockout stage · Round of 32

Single elimination from here. A draw after 90 minutes goes to extra time and, if still level, penalties. Winners advance to the Round of 16 (July 4 to 7).

Sunday, June 28 · Round of 32

R32South Africa0 : 1Canada

Canada are the first team into the Round of 16. Stephen Eustáquio's stoppage-time half-volley (90+2') settled a tense, low-scoring runners-up derby at SoFi Stadium that had looked bound for extra time. It was Canada's first-ever World Cup knockout win; South Africa, in their first knockout tie, go out at the first hurdle. Alphonso Davies returned from his hamstring layoff off the bench. Canada next meet the winner of Netherlands vs Morocco in Houston on July 4. Three more round-of-32 ties follow on June 29: Netherlands vs Morocco, Brazil vs Japan, and Germany vs Paraguay.

Monday, June 29 · Round of 32

R32Netherlands2 : 1Morocco
R32Brazil2 : 1Japan
R32Germany3 : 1Paraguay

Three more sides reached the Round of 16. The Netherlands beat Morocco 2-1 in Monterrey, Cody Gakpo (28') and Brian Brobbey (61') putting them in control before Youssef En-Nesyri (74') set up a nervy finish; the Dutch held on to book a last-16 meeting with Canada in Houston on July 4. Brazil edged a dangerous Japan 2-1 in Houston, Matheus Cunha (35') and Vinícius Júnior (58') overturning an early scare before Kaoru Mitoma (80') made it tense; Brazil next face the winner of Ivory Coast vs Norway. Germany beat Paraguay 3-1 in Boston through Florian Wirtz (22'), Kai Havertz (40') and Jamal Musiala (66'), Julio Enciso (78') replying to leave Nagelsmann's side still without a clean sheet at the tournament; Germany meet the winner of France vs Sweden in Philadelphia on July 4. The Round of 32 continues June 30 with France vs Sweden, Ivory Coast vs Norway, and Mexico vs Ecuador.

Tuesday, June 30 · Round of 32

R32Ivory Coast1 : 3Norway
R32France2 : 0Sweden
R32Mexico1 : 1Ecuador

Three more places in the Round of 16 were settled, with the co-hosts among the fallers. Norway beat the Ivory Coast 3-1 in Dallas, Erling Haaland (22', 58') scoring either side of Amad Diallo's (40') equalizer before Antonio Nusa (74') sealed it; Norway go on to meet Brazil in New Jersey on July 5. France were comfortable 2-0 winners over Sweden at MetLife Stadium, Kylian Mbappé (30') and Michael Olise (71') on target to set up a Round-of-16 showdown with Germany in Philadelphia on July 4. The night's shock came at the Estadio Azteca, where Ecuador knocked out co-hosts Mexico on penalties (4-2) after a 1-1 draw; Santiago Giménez (55') put Mexico ahead before Enner Valencia (78') levelled, and Ecuador held their nerve from the spot to end the group winners' tournament and set up a last-16 tie with the winner of England vs DR Congo. The Round of 32 continues July 1 with England vs DR Congo, Belgium vs Senegal, and USA vs Bosnia & Herzegovina.
